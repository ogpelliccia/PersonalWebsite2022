This page contains information and links about some of my interests and pasttimes.

My Interests

When I am not busy with schoolwork, my main interests include computers, video games, and cars.

Computers

Before I was even in middle school, I first got into computers when I learned about virtualization. Because of that, I became interested in older computers, having played around with MS-DOS, which was the predecessor to Windows, and the software of that era. I also have a great interest in building computers, having built a few systems, including the one that I wrote this website on. In recent years, I have also gained an interest in programming, having learned Python in high school and C# during my time at LCCC. I also plan on becoming a programmer or database administrator after college.

Newegg.com This is the go-to place for buying computer parts and other electronics
PCem This is my favorite program for emulating old computer hardware. It emulates x86 systems from the 1980s and 1990s.
The Internet Archive's Vintage Software Collection This part of the Internet Archive has lots of old, obscure, and interesting software and games for many different systems. (most of this content is in the sub-collections.)


Video Games

Video games have been a part of my life for as long as I can remember. I've played many games throughout my life, but I have always favored the Simulation genre, with my current favorites being BeamNG.Drive, Automation, The Sims, and Cities: Skylines, to name a few. I do also like action games like the Grand Theft Auto Series and Counter Strike: Global Offensive. Of course, my system of choice is the PC, since I like the freedom and exclusive titles that computers have to offer.

Steam Steam is my go-to marketplace for all of my PC games. I have bought many games from here and I believe that it's essential for PC gaming.
BeamNG.Drive One of my favorite games to play. It is essentially a vehicle simulator where you can drive and crash cars. You can do alot in this game.
Automation This is another game that I recommend you check out. It is supposed to be a car company tycoon game, but I mainly use it to build cars that I can drive in BeamNG.Drive.


Cars

I was never really a car guy unitl a few years ago, when I discovered Automation. I believed that it opened the door for my interest in cars. Since I can't really afford the cars I want, I could just dream about the cars that I would love to buy if I had the money. My style of cars has leaned more towards old American coupes, usually from the 1970s through the 1990s. Three cars that I would buy if I had the money (from left to right) would be the 1977 Ford Thunderbird (in white and red), the 1987 Buick Regal Grand National, and the 1995 Chevy Corvette ZR1.

Automobile Catalog This is a very useful site to lookup information about a certain car model, such as 0-60, MPG, Power, etc.
Car and Driver A news site with many new articles relating to cars and trucks.
Facebook Marketplace While not exactly a car site, I like looking at the cars that pop up in my feed.
